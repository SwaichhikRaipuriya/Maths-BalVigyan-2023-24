"Introduction to coding"


‘Coding’ or ‘Computer Programming’ involves creating step-by-step instructions that have a logical structure.
Inputting these instructions means a computer can execute a program, or a compiled set of programs, called software applications

When you write a computer program using a high-level language like Python, you are instructing a computer what to do for a specific purpose. This instruction is then translated into machine codes and binary bits of information by the computer. Although you can use ready-made and user-friendly applications to do certain tasks, there are many cases where you’ll need to customize your programs or software applications, or even develop your own.

Computer coding can be classified into four major types:
• Imperative or Procedural (examples: COBOL, FORTRAN, C, etc.)
• Functional (examples: Haskell, Scala, Clojure, etc.)
• Logical (examples: Prolog, SQL, etc.)
• Object-oriented (examples: Java, C++, Python, etc.)

Many high-level programming languages use logic and functions
to define instructions.


Coding: A Gateway to Limitless Opportunities

In the 21st century, coding has emerged as a vital skill that extends far beyond the confines of computer screens. The benefits of coding reach into various aspects of our lives, offering a multitude of advantages that contribute to personal growth, professional success, and societal progress.

1.Enhanced Problem-Solving Skills: Coding is, at its core, a problem-solving endeavour. Engaging in coding activities sharpens analytical thinking, encouraging individuals to break down complex challenges into manageable components. This honing of problem-solving skills is a valuable asset applicable in every facet of life.

2.Empowerment through Creativity: Coding is a creative process that empowers individuals to bring their ideas to life in the digital realm. Whether designing a website, crafting a mobile app, or developing a game, programming provides a platform for self-expression and innovation, fostering a sense of creative empowerment.

3.Global Connectivity and Collaboration: The ability to design programming codes transcends geographical boundaries. In our interconnected world, coding serves as a universal language that facilitates collaboration and communication across borders. Programmers can contribute to global projects, participate in open-source communities, and collaborate with like-minded individuals from diverse backgrounds.

Mathematical operations in QR Codes

Introduction

Since their 1994 invention by the DENSOCorporation in Japan, QR codes have provided a widely used method for quickly accessing information. Originally, QR codes were used for parts manufacturing inventory tracking, but they can now be found on advertisements, web pages, business cards, any many other mediums. QR codes are notable for their long-term stability and higher capacity for storage than other bar codes. However, like other physical means of storing data, QR codes are prone to errors. Reed-Solomon codes provide a mechanism for ensuring that QR code scanners can reliably process information when errors occur.
